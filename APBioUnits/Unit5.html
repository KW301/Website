Key Concepts

Law of Segregation : The two alleles for a trait separate during the formation of gametes

Law of Independent Assortment : Genes for different traits are inherited independently of each other

Linked genes are genes that are located on the same chromosome and tend to be inherited together

Law of Dominance : In a heterozygote, the dominant allele (usually represented by a uppercase letter) will be expressed over the recessive allele (usually represented by a lowercase letter)

Autosomal Chromosome : A chromosome not involved in gender

Autosomal Recessive Disorder : A genetic disorder that requires two copies of a recessive allele to be expressed

Autosomal Dominant Disorder : A genetic disorder that requires only one copy of a dominant allele to be expressed

Nondisjuction Errors : The failure of homologous chromosomes or sister chromatids to separate properly during cell division

Allele : A variant of a gene for one character

Multiple Alleles : A gene that has more than two alleles

Genotype : The genetic makeup of an organism

Phenotype : The physical characteristics of an organism

Homozygous : Having two identical alleles for a trait (2 dominant or 2 recessive)

Heterozygous : Having two different alleles for a trait (1 dominant, 1 recessive)

Karyotype : a visual representation of an organism's chromosomes

P1 : The parent generation in a genetic cross

F1 : The first generation of offspring in a genetic cross

F2 : The second generation of offspring in a genetic cross

Incomplete Dominance : A type of inheritance where neither allele is completely dominant over the other, resulting in a phenotype that is a blend of the two traits

Monohybrid Cross : Involves one traits from both heterozygous parents resulting in a 3:1 ratio of dominant to recessive phenotypes

Dihybrid Cross : Involves two traits from both heterozygous parents resulting in a 9:3:3:1 ratio of pheno,types

Non-Nuclear Inheritance : When the offspring only gets the DNA from one parent rather than a mix of both (Humans only get mitochondrial DNA from their mother)

Codominance : Occurs in a heterozygous organism, when both alleles are expressed

Polygenic Traits : traits that are affected by more than one gene (EX. eye color)

Epistasis : When the expression of a gene affects the expression of another gene

Pleiotropy : When a single gene affects multiple traits of an organism

Sex Determination : The process which determines sex for a organism. The 23rd chromosome determines the sex of humans, with XX chromosomes resulting in females and XY chromosomes resulting in males

X Inactivation : One of the two X chromosomes in females is randomly inactivated and remains as a Barr body

Barr body : A condensed inactivated X chromosome

Pedigrees : A family tree used to track the inheritance of a trait
