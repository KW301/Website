Behavioral Ecology : Study of how animals interact with their environment and with each other

Ethology : Study of animal behavior in natural environments

Types of Animal Learning

  • Fixed-Action Pattern : A unchanged sequence of behaviors triggered by a specific stimulus

  • Habituation : A loss of response to a unimportant stimulus that provides no benefit

  • Imprinting : A innate behavior that occurs during a critical period of development, often during the early stages of life

  • Associative Learning : When a animal links a stimulus with a response that leads to either a punishment or reward

  • Operant Conditioning : A type of learning where behavior is modified by its consequences

  • Insight Learning : The ability to solve problems through comprehension rather than trial and error

  • Observational Learning : Learning by observing others first

Types of Animal Movement

Kinesis : Change in speed of movement in response to a stimulus; faster in bad environments and slower in good environments

Migration : Cycle of movement over long distances over a period of time

Taxis : Movement in response to a stimulus; can be towards or away from the stimulus

Animal Behaviors

  • Agonistic Behavior : Conflict behavior over access to resources. Often it which animal can make their other into submission
  • Domainance Heiarchies : Ranking of power among the members of the group. Since the members know the order, it would reduce conflicts over resources
  • Territoriality : Defense of territory to keep others out
  • Altruistic Behavior : When a organism helps others at their own expense
  • Reciprocal Altruism : Animals behave altruistically in exchange for future benefits
  • Foraging : Feeding behavior of animals
  • Optimal Foraging : Natural selection favors strageties that maximize energy gain and cost of energy
